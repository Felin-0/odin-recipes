Pilau

Description

Kenyan pilau rice is a simple dish of rice flavoured with meat stock and spices and sometimes cooked with vegetables. This aromatic rice dish is commonly served at parties and occasions, usually with kachumbari (tomato & cucumber salad). Similar to jollof rice in West Africa, it is always present on Eid, Christmas and other special occasions. Kenyan pilau rice is also known as Swahili pilau rice and East African pilau rice.

Pilau dish is common in India and the Middle Eastern countries, each with its way of preparing it. The East African pilau rice is common in countries around the Swahili coast like Kenya and Tanzania; hence, it is also called Swahili pilau. Although there are a lot of Indian influences in the local cuisine of the countries around the Swahili coast, The Kenyan or Swahili pilau is more similar in appearance to the Middle Eastern version with a light brown colour than the Indian pilau with bright yellow colour from the addition of turmeric.

Ingredients

Here is a list of the ingredients you need for beef pilau

  • 500g of beef
  • 2 onions
  • 4 pieces of garlic
  • Ginger, 5cm in size or thumb-sized
  • 2 small bay leaves
  • Salt
  • 1 tsp Ground cloves
  • 1/2tsp Ground cumin
  • 1 tsp Ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp Ground cardamom
  • Pepper flakes
  • 2 cups of rice

Directions:

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make it:

  1. Peel the onions and garlic cloves.

  2. Wash the beef and add into a pan. Slice in one onion and 2 garlic cloves with the ginger. Add bay leaves, with a cup of water and some salt to taste.

  3. Cook until the beef is tender, then remove from heat. Keep the meet chunks and stock to one side.

  4. Chop the remaining onion and garlic.

  5. Wash the rice and repeat until the water runs clear.

  6. In another pan, heat the oil under low heat. Add the onions into the pot and cook until the onions start to caramelize and become brown. You should stir the onions with a wooden spoon continuously to prevent burning.

  7. Pour in the garlic and the ground spices. The brownish colour comes from the brown spices. Stir for 30 secs.

  8. Add the rice into the pot. Pour in the stock and meat chunks. Add more water so there is enough to cook the rice (read the rice pack instructions). Taste for salt and add more if needed.

  9. Cover the pot, and allow the rice to cook until all the moisture is absorbed.
